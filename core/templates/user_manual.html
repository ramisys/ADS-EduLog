EduLog User Manual

Complete guide to using EduLog for Teachers, Students, and Parents

Getting Started

Creating an Account

To get started with EduLog, you need to create an account based on your role:

Step 1: Click on the "Sign Up" button on the login page
Step 2: Select your role (Teacher, Student, or Parent)
Step 3: Fill in your personal information
Step 4: Create a secure password
Step 5: Submit the form and verify your email (if required)

Logging In

After creating your account, you can log in using:

  • Your username
  • Your email address
  • Your ID number (Teacher ID, Student ID, etc.)
Note: Make sure to select the correct role tab (Teacher, Student, or Parent) before logging in.

Password Recovery

If you forget your password:

Step 1: Click "Forgot password?" on the login page
Step 2: Enter your email address
Step 3: Check your email for password reset instructions
Step 4: Follow the link to create a new password

Teacher Guide

Dashboard Overview

As a teacher, your dashboard provides:

  • Overview of all your assigned sections
  • Quick access to student attendance records
  • Performance analytics and grade summaries
  • Notifications about student attendance issues
  • Recent activity feed

Managing Students

Viewing Student List

Navigate to the "Students" section from the sidebar menu. You'll see a list of all students in your assigned sections with their basic information.

Viewing Student Details

Click on any student's name to view their detailed profile, including:
  • Attendance history
  • Grade breakdown by subject
  • Performance trends
  • Assessment scores

Attendance Management

Recording Attendance

Step 1: Go to "Attendance" from the sidebar
Step 2: Select the section and subject
Step 3: Choose the date
Step 4: Mark each student as Present, Absent, or Late
Step 5: Save the attendance record

Viewing Attendance Reports

You can view attendance reports by:

  • Individual student
  • Section/Class
  • Subject
  • Date range

Grade Management

Creating Assessments

Step 1: Navigate to "Grades" or "Assessments"
Step 2: Click "Add New Assessment"
Step 3: Fill in the details:
  • Assessment name
  • Category (Activity, Quiz, Project, Exam)
  • Subject
  • Maximum score
  • Date
  • Term (Midterm/Final)
Step 4: Save the assessment

Recording Student Scores

Step 1: Select an assessment from the list
Step 2: Click "Add Scores" or "Record Scores"
Step 3: Enter scores for each student
Step 4: Save the scores

Setting Category Weights

You can customize how different assessment categories contribute to the final grade:

Step 1: Go to "Category Weights" in the Grades section
Step 2: Select a subject
Step 3: Set percentage weights for:
  • Activities
  • Quizzes
  • Projects
  • Exams
Step 4: Ensure weights total 100%
Step 5: Save the weights
Important: Category weights must sum to exactly 100% for the system to calculate grades correctly.

Notifications

You'll receive automatic notifications for:

  • Students with consecutive absences
  • Students at risk (low attendance or grades)
  • Students showing improvement
  • Important updates and announcements

Frequently Asked Questions

General Questions

Q: How do I reset my password?

A: Click "Forgot password?" on the login page, enter your email, and follow the instructions sent to your email.

Q: Can I change my email address?

A: Yes, you can update your email address in your profile settings after logging in.

Q: Is my data secure?

A: Yes, EduLog uses enterprise-grade security measures to protect all user data. Your information is encrypted and stored securely.

Teacher Questions

Q: How do I add a new assessment?

A: Go to the Grades section, click "Add New Assessment", fill in the required details, and save.

Q: Can I edit assessment scores after recording them?

A: Yes, you can edit scores at any time. Navigate to the assessment, click on the score you want to change, and update it.

Q: How are final grades calculated?

A: Final grades are calculated using weighted averages based on the category weights you set for each subject. The system multiplies each category's average by its weight and sums them up.

Student Questions

Q: Why can't I see my grades for a subject?

A: Grades may not be visible if your teacher hasn't recorded scores yet, or if the assessment hasn't been completed. Contact your teacher for more information.

Q: How do I know if I'm at risk of failing?

A: You'll receive notifications if your grades or attendance fall below acceptable levels. You can also check your dashboard for performance warnings.

Parent Questions

Q: Can I see grades for multiple children?

A: Yes, if you have multiple children registered, you can switch between them using the dropdown menu on your dashboard.

Q: How often is attendance updated?

A: Attendance is updated in real-time as teachers record it. You'll receive notifications immediately when your child is marked absent or late.
